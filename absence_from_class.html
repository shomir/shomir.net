Absence From Class

I sometimes send this page as a quick reply when students contact me about being absent from class. I appreciate your concern about keeping up, but if I sent you this page, it's likely that one of these three scenarios apply:

  1. You're concerned about what you'll miss: Remember the instructions in the syllabus. Instead of contacting me, you should check with another student in the class.
  2. If I've announced a grading system for in-class participation, you're concerned about missing an opportunity: You can't make up graded in-class participation if you're absent, but when I grade it I use a system that permits you to miss several opportunities without losing any points. It's unlikely that you needed to contact me.
  3. You're concerned about missing a graded in-class activity that isn't participation (e.g., a presentation or a test): Please reply with a clearer statement about the graded non-participation activity that you're missing. If I sent you this page, I didn't understand your concern.

I prefer that students don't notify me about absences from class unless #3 above applies. It's more efficient for both of us, and it's better for you in the long run to be familiar with the instructions in the syllabus and the course materials.

Some faculty members ignore emails like yours, but I felt bad doing that, so I created this page to send instead. Replying with this page also avoids the possibility of students misunderstanding their responsibilities in my classes.
