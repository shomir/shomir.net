Example CV for PhD Students

This page is part of my advice pages.

I created an example CV in LaTeX for PhD students. Rather than a fictional CV (which could be unrealistic) or my actual CV as a PhD student (which could have been better), this is a complete overhaul of my graduate school CV using my current knowledge as a professor.

I release the following files:

  • The LaTeX code, based upon an earlier template
  • An annotated version of the CV with extensive advice in endnotes on the last page
  • A separate document containing just the endnotes (in case you'd like to read them side-by-side with the annotated CV)
  • A "clean" version of the CV without endnotes

As the endnotes explain, this CV represents me in Fall 2009. I was four years into the Computer Science PhD program at University of Maryland and two years away from graduating. I had completed my comprehensive exam and PhD proposal in April of that year. To avoid confusing web crawlers or out-of-context human readers, I've substituted in anagrams of my name (hence the fictitious Wilhom Rosins) and all other people's names. I've also rephrased all of our paper titles.

Remember the difference between a CV and a resume: for a PhD student, the CV demonstrates your scholarly profile and trajectory, while the resume demonstrates your preparation to work on specific kinds of tasks. The CV is often best when applying for awards, fellowships, and jobs within academia. The resume is best for most jobs in industry. If you're uncertain which to provide for an opportunity, just ask.

Note that I am a computer scientist in the USA, and CV norms may vary by discipline and location.
