For All Students

I created these guides to help students understand often-unwritten customs and procedures in academia.

The Guide for Interacting With Faculty is for students (especially first-year undergraduates, but also others) who want to learn about expectations that university faculty have for them.

The Guide for Scholarly Writing is for students who are writing manuscripts to submit for publication.

The Guide for Citations and References is for students who are new to using citations and references, or need to refresh their memory.

For Prospective or Current Members of My Lab

The Guide for Joining My Lab is for prospective students and visitors who are interested in doing research with me.

The Guide for Lab Resources is for my current research advisees.

More Guidance

The Guide for the Tenure-Track Job Market in Computer/Information Sciences is for people (students and others) who are preparing to apply for faculty positions.

Work-life balance is important. I wrote Thoughts on Photography to share the creative process behind the pictures throughout these guides.

I encourage my students to read my guides, but reading does not replace human interaction. You're welcome to contact me.

Finally, Need Help? is a page I sometimes send as a reply to prospective research advisees who need help formulating their questions. I encourage other faculty to reply with a page like this when necessary, rather than ignoring prospectives' emails.
