IRFP

From August 2013 through July 2014 I was a visiting postdoctoral researcher at the University of Edinburgh in Scotland. I received funding for this trip from the National Science Foundation's International Research Fellowship Program (IRFP). IRFP was a great program, open to applicants in all NSF-funded areas of research, but it was discontinued immediately after I received my grant. NSF still funds some international postdoctoral research through other programs.

You can see some of my pictures from Scotland in an album on Flickr.

EAPSI

Twice during graduate school I received grants from the National Science Foundation's East Asia and Pacific Summer Institutes (EAPSI). These grants enabled me to spend two summers (June-August) abroad working on projects with researchers in Australia (2009) and Singapore (2010). Both summers were signature experiences in my graduate education: the Australia project served as an early stage of my dissertation work, and the Singapore project gave me a wider view of my research topic and its applications. Additionally, I continue to benefit from the networking I did while abroad.

These trips also provided many opportunities for sightseeing and adventures in distant parts of the world that I had long wanted to visit. In the sections below, mouse over the links to see pictures, and click to see them larger.

2009: Australia (and Singapore)

12 flights, 9 airports, 31,623 miles

I spent the summer (or south-centric winter) of 2009 with the Centre for Language Technology at Macquarie University in Sydney, Australia. On the weekends, I saw the usual sights, went hiking along the coast in Manly and Royal National Park, attended a Sydney Roosters game, visited many museums, rode a lot of CityRail trains, and saw many distinctive Australian birds. I took a cheesy picture of myself at a certain streetcorner in downtown Sydney to see if anyone could identify its significance (hint: the phone stand once was a booth). The EAPSI orientation was in Canberra, so I flew there for a weekend visit early in my stay. Later, I took two weekend trips with other EAPSI participants stationed in Sydney: one to the Blue Mountains, and another to the Hunter Valley and Port Stephens.

I also flew to Singapore for a few days to attend a conference. I did not have much time for sightseeing during the conference, but I got out a little in the evenings. I also spent some time exploring on my final day there: I visited Little India and Chinatown, then took a train to a bus to a bumboat to the island of Pulau Ubin, where I rented a bicycle, pedaled out into the mangrove forest, saw monkeys and wild boars, made a wrong turn, ended up on a path that gradually became impassable for the bike, dismounted, walked a little further, and took this picture. I then hurried to the airport to catch my overnight flight back to Australia. The next morning I landed in Brisbane, missed my connecting flight to Sydney (a long story), and decided to ride the train into the city to do some impromptu sightseeing. I was rebooked on a later flight onward to Sydney, which I did not miss.

After the EAPSI program officially ended, I visited more of Australia before returning home. I flew out to Alice Springs, went hiking in McDonnell National Park, drove across the desert to Yulara, saw Uluru and Kata Tjuta, flew from there to Cairns, snorkeled on the Great Barrier Reef, made a day trip north into tropical rainforests, and finally returned to Sydney to catch my flights back home.


2010: Singapore (and many other places)

11 flights, 10 airports, 24,131 miles

I spent the duration of the EAPSI 2010 program with the Web Information Retrieval / Natural Language Processing Group (WING) at the National University of Singapore. I also visited several other places in an extended itinerary. My summer began with a flight to...

Los Angeles. I attended the NAACL-HLT conference, where I presented a paper. I stayed one day after the conference ended to go sightseeing in Hollywood and Long Beach, and late that evening boarded a plane to...

Hong Kong. I intentionally scheduled a layover of several hours so that I could leave the airport and go sightseeing. Although I spent some time in Kowloon and Central Hong Kong, the highlight of my visit was the view from Victoria Peak. Afterward, I continued onward to...

Singapore. It was my second time in the country, since I had visited briefly in the summer of 2009. I arrived a week before the official EAPSI program started and began work early. As in Australia, the weekends were valuable: I visited the Chinese Gardens in Jurong, the National Botanical Gardens, several museums, and the so-claimed Southernmost Point in Mainland Asia. Central Singapore was especially beautiful at night. I also led an expedition of fellow EAPSI participants on my return to Pulau Ubin: we biked across the rural island, encountered many animals (including monkeys, wild boars, and mudskippers), and took this picture with a local landmark. A few of us were able to attend the National Day Parade Preview. One weekend I took a day-trip via high-speed ferry to...

The Riau Islands of Indonesia. We (EAPSI participants) started in the small city of Tanjung Pinang, then took a small ferry boat to Penyengat Island and then Senggarang, a large community built on stilts above the shallow water and mud of a tidal plain. On another weekend, a few of us visited...

Kuala Lumpur, the capital of Malaysia. Points of interest from our visit included the Batu Caves (home to a Hindu temple and many mischievous monkeys), the KL Tower, the remnants of British rule, and the National Museum. We also happened to see a free performance of movie music composed by Jamil Sulong. One night I took a set of photos to create this stitched-together image. On my final weekend staying in Singapore, a few of us rode a bus across the Johor-Singapore Causeway for a day-trip to...

Johor Bahru, Malaysia, where I didn't take any interesting pictures. After my EAPSI stay ended, one other participant and I left Singapore by plane for...

Close-up of my destinations in Southeast Asia

Bali, Indonesia's "island of the gods". With some help from a local connection (a friend of a friend back in the US) we traveled across the island and saw rice terraces, picturesque beaches, and many lively street processions. We hiked up and around the crater rim of Mount Batur, an active volcano. We also visited the traditional Bali attractions in the south, including Tanah Lot, Padang Padang, and the Kecak Fire Dance at Ulu Watu. Afterward, I continued alone on a flight to...

Bangkok, Thailand's legendary capital. I visited many temples, including Wat Tramit and Wat Phra Kaew, as well as the Royal Palace. I saw the city's Little India and Chinatown. I puzzled over the role of squid in Thai cuisine. Then, I began the long return home with an overnight flight to...

Tokyo, where I scheduled a long layover once again so I could leave the airport and explore. I spent most of my time in the East Gardens of the Imperial Palace. Most of the flowers were past bloom, but I did see some colorful insects. From there I flew overnight (again) to...

Chicago, where I intended to meet a friend for coffee in the airport terminal. Unfortunately my flight left Tokyo very late, and my friend was unable to meet me when I arrived. I missed my original flight onward but was rebooked on a later one to...

Pittsburgh, where I visited someone special and slept on land (i.e., not in flight) for the first time since Bangkok two days ago. A few days later, I finally returned (on the shortest flight of the summer) to...

Washington, DC, to resume to my usual graduate studies.
